Biography

I am a post-doctoral researcher at the Robotics, Perception, and Learning Lab and the Center for Autonomous Systems. My research interests include autonomous intelligent systems, planning and learning for dynamical systems, robotic grasping and manipulation, and robotic perception. Currently, I am working with the research group headed by Danica Kragic that focuses on autonomous robotic grasping and manipulation problems.

Previously, I worked with the social robotics research group of Kai Arras at Freiburg University, Germany, researching range-based people tracking, audio-based perception, and learning for motion planning in human-populated environments. After that, as a PhD. student of Danica Kragic at KTH, I researched topology-based caging with Danica Kragic, grasp synthesis with Kaiyu Hang, and predictive state representations for robotics with Carl Henrik Ek and Yasemin Bekiroglu. My mentors were Diego Tipaldi and Luciano Spinello.
